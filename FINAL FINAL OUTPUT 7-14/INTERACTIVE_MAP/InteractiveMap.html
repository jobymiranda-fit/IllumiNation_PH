National Electrification Efforts

Information on government and NGO efforts to achieve 100% electrification across the Philippines.

National Overview

An estimated 1.8 to 3.6 million households remain without power, primarily in Geographically Isolated and Disadvantaged Areas (GIDAs). The National Electrification Administration (NEA) leads the effort through its Sitio Electrification Program (SEP).

2025 Budget: The NEA has a budget of ₱3.6 billion for this purpose.

Efforts by Island Group

Mindanao (₱2.5 billion budget)

Mindanao is the top priority. Key focus areas include:

  • BARMM: Lowest electrification rate (Lanao del Sur, Maguindanao, Sulu, Tawi-Tawi).
  • Other Regions: Northern Mindanao, Davao Region, Soccsksargen, and Caraga.

Luzon (₱701.2 million budget)

Efforts target mountainous and remote islands. Key focus areas include:

  • CAR & Cagayan Valley: NGOs like SIBAT are active with micro-hydro projects.
  • MIMAROPA & Bicol: Groups like ICSC provide solar solutions in Palawan, Quezon, etc.

Visayas (₱462.6 million budget)

Focus on interior mountains and smaller islands. Key areas include:

  • Western & Central Visayas: Antique, Negros Occidental, and Siquijor.
  • Eastern Visayas: Samar and Leyte, focusing on resilient energy systems.
Role of LGUs & NGOs

LGUs (Local Government Units): Crucial for identifying communities, coordinating with electric co-ops, and facilitating projects.

NGOs (Non-Governmental Organizations): Specialize in deploying off-grid renewable energy. Groups like SIBAT, ICSC, and Project Liwanag empower communities with their own solar or micro-hydro systems, complementing government efforts.
